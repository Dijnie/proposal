## Impact Review (5/5 Stars)
**Rating**: GREAT

**Strengths:**
- **Truly innovative DeSci vertical**: First credible blockchain-based chemistry data infrastructure addressing real scientific pain points (siloed data, lack of verification, no incentive to share failed experiments)
- **Clear ecosystem benefits**: Introduces Cardano to scientific/academic communities, showcases eUTxO + Haskell strengths, creates reusable primitives (CIP-68 schemas, chimia-core library)
- **Measurable on-chain metrics**: Specific targets (≥200 reaction-step UTxOs, ≥25 unique wallets, ≥50 ORD reactions validated, ≥5 pilot labs)
- **Multiplicative potential**: Infrastructure enables future builders (explorers, analytics, IP marketplaces, DeFi for scientific IP, DAO-funded lab work)
- **Real-world integration**: Uses Open Reaction Database (ORD) with 4M+ reactions, not toy examples
- **Addresses significant problem**: Chemistry data locked in proprietary formats costs industry billions in duplicated research

**Weaknesses:**
- Community adoption risk: Scientists/labs unfamiliar with blockchain may struggle with wallet UX, transaction fees, and conceptual shift
- Long-term sustainability unclear: No post-grant revenue model or maintenance plan mentioned
- Limited immediate Cardano TVL/transaction impact: Primary benefit is strategic positioning rather than near-term network activity

- **Technical complexity underestimated**: Type-safe chemistry validation in Plutus, cross-chain message passing, CIP-68 implementation, and ORD parsing simultaneously is high-risk

- **Audit budget concern**: ₳25k for auditing Plutus + EVM + FVM contracts may be insufficient given complexity

**Critical Gaps:**
- No CVs/portfolios for unnamed team members
- No letters of intent from the "≥5 pilot labs"
- No existing relationship with ORD maintainers mentioned
- Monad is a nascent chain - integration risk if their testnet changes

## Overall Assessment

ChimiaDAO represents one of the most technically ambitious and intellectually compelling proposals in Fund15. The vision of bringing verifiable, on-chain chemistry data to Cardano addresses a genuine scientific problem (₳billions in duplicated research) while positioning Cardano as the trust layer for DeSci. The integration of Haskell ADTs with Plutus validators, type-safe chemistry invariants, and CIP-68 standardization demonstrates deep technical sophistication that aligns perfectly with Cardano's strengths.

However, the proposal suffers from execution risk. The team section is concerning: only one person (Cristina Castro) is identified, yet the budget allocates ₳120k for multiple specialized engineers (Cardano-Haskell, EVM/Monad, Filecoin). Without confirmed hires, partners, or advisors, the feasibility of delivering across four complex technology stacks (Cardano, Monad, Filecoin, ORD integration) in 8 months is questionable. The existing ETHGlobal prototype is impressive proof-of-concept, but scaling to production-grade, audited, multi-chain infrastructure requires a proven team.

The cross-chain architecture also raises value-for-money concerns. While Cardano serves as the "canonical truth layer," substantial budget (₳60k in personnel alone) goes to Monad/Filecoin integration. Voters should ask whether a Cardano-only MVP (Milestones 1-2, costing ₳80k) would provide clearer ecosystem value before expanding to external chains. The ₳25k audit budget seems insufficient for three contract environments, and the ₳15k contingency is thin for R&D.

**Recommendation**: This proposal has exceptional potential but needs team de-risking. Ideal scenario: fund conditionally upon team confirmations (named developers, pilot lab letters of intent) or reduce scope to Cardano-only MVP (₳80-100k) with cross-chain expansion in a follow-on proposal. If the team materializes as described and pilot labs commit, this could be a flagship DeSci achievement for Cardano. Without those confirmations, it's high-risk.

**Strengths Summary:**
1. Addresses real ₳billion scientific problem with novel technical approach
2. Creates reusable Cardano infrastructure (CIP-68 schemas, chimia-core library)
3. Strong on-chain success metrics and measurable outcomes
4. Positions Cardano in academic/scientific circles (long-term strategic value)

**Risks Summary:**
1. Team composition unclear beyond founder (no confirmed engineers/advisors)
2. Multi-chain scope ambitious for 8-month timeline

4. No pilot lab commitments or ORD maintainer relationships documented
5. Cross-chain dependencies (Monad testnet stability) create external risks
