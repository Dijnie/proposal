## Impact Review (4/5 Stars)
**Rating**: GOOD

**Strengths:**
- **Novel use case**: World's first space industry carbon oracle - completely greenfield application with no comparable blockchain-based system
- **Real ecosystem expansion**: Demonstrates Cardano's viability beyond DeFi/gaming into industrial IoT, real-world data oracles, and ESG compliance infrastructure
- **Clear market gap**: 261 launches in 2024 generating ~50,000 tons CO₂ with no standardized verification system - addresses genuine industry problem
- **Academic rigor**: UN-adopted framework (Dr. Makane Faye, UNECA) + UNLP certification provides credibility and methodology validation
- **Multiple innovation vectors**: ZK proofs for IP protection, automated offset certification, data-as-a-service marketplace, green finance infrastructure
- **High visibility potential**: Entry point into $500B+ space economy with mission-critical infrastructure appeal
- **Measurable outcomes**: 50+ oracle updates, 5+ datasets, 100+ UI interactions, 50+ community testers, 90-95% accuracy validation
- **Reusable infrastructure**: Open-source components (Apache 2.0) enable other builders to adopt for real-world data applications
- **Revenue model**: Data marketplace creating new asset class (verified space emissions) with 70/30 revenue split for launch providers

**Weaknesses:**
- **Adoption uncertainty**: No confirmed launch provider mentioned by name - reliance on "launch partner under NDA" creates execution risk
- **Market timing**: Space industry ESG regulations still nascent - may be building infrastructure before demand materializes
- **Scale limitations**: 261 launches/year is relatively small market compared to aviation (38M+ flights/year) - limits near-term impact
- **Cardano positioning**: Unclear competitive advantage vs. other chains for this specific use case - why not Ethereum, Polygon, or Polkadot?
- **Impact measurement**: On-chain metrics focus on testnet activity, not real-world adoption (e.g., no target for mainnet launch providers onboarded)

## Overall Assessment

This proposal tackles a genuinely novel problem - bringing verifiable rocket launch emissions data on-chain through a Cardano oracle - with strong academic backing and a well-articulated vision for industrial IoT integration. The team demonstrates strategic thinking around ZK proofs for IP protection, data marketplace revenue models, and green finance infrastructure that could position Cardano as a serious player in the $500B+ space economy.

However, execution risk is significant. While the team has impressive credentials on paper (UN diplomat advisor, international lawyer, government liaison), there is **no verifiable evidence of Cardano-specific development experience**. The GitHub repos show data science and general development work, but not Plutus smart contracts, oracle infrastructure, or blockchain integration. The ambitious technical scope - integrating AWS, IoT sensors, rocket telemetry, ZK-SNARKs, and Cardano oracles in 5 months - would challenge even experienced blockchain teams, and this team appears to be learning Cardano development while building.

The reliance on an unnamed "launch partner under NDA" creates existential risk: without real telemetry data, the entire value proposition collapses to a theoretical prototype with simulated data. UNLP partnership and UN framework provide methodological credibility, but don't guarantee technical execution.

Budget allocation raises questions: 32.5% going to founders for non-technical roles (product/operations) seems premium, especially when the core technical work (smart contracts, oracle, ZK proofs) is allocated 35% (70,000 ADA). The 40,000 ADA buffer (20%) is prudent but also suggests uncertainty about technical complexity.

**Recommendation**: This proposal represents a **high-risk, high-reward** bet on expanding Cardano's real-world utility. If successful, it delivers exceptional ecosystem value and visibility. However, voters should weigh the lack of verifiable Cardano experience, partner dependency risks, and ambitious technical scope against the 200,000 ADA investment. A funded outcome should include strict milestone verification and willingness to course-correct if early milestones reveal technical bottlenecks.

**Key Questions for Proposer:**
1. Can you provide evidence of prior Plutus/Aiken smart contract development by team members?
2. Is the "launch partner under NDA" contractually committed to providing telemetry data? Can you name the partner confidentially to reviewers?
3. What is your fallback plan if ZK proof implementation proves infeasible within 5 months?
4. How will you ensure oracle decentralization and prevent single-point-of-failure risks?
5. What is the specific scope of work for Sukhmeet S/Feooh team, and is their cost included in the 70,000 ADA dev budget or the 40,000 ADA buffer?
